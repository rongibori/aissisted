# **STRATEGIC INSIGHT (FOR YOU)**

This slide does something extremely important:

It answers silently:

👉 “How much should I invest?”  
 👉 “How big can this get?”  
 👉 “Is this pattern proven?”

Let’s walk through each comparable, focusing on seed, time to valuation, total growth, and subscription milestones.

First, Hims & Hers:

* Seed: They raised about $3 million in their seed round back in 2017\.  
* Time to Valuation: It took roughly 4 years to reach a $1.6 billion valuation with their SPAC IPO in 2021\.  
* Total Growth: After the seed, they raised about $40 million in Series A shortly after, and then went public. Their revenue hit $1.5 billion in about 7 years.  
* Subscription Growth: They started with a small set of customers, but by year 1, they were focusing on early adopters (a few thousand). By year 5, they scaled to over 2 million subscribers, fueled by aggressive DTC (direct-to-consumer) and brand expansion.

Second, Function of Beauty:

* Seed: They raised about $9.5 million, including Y Combinator, in 2015\.  
* Time to Valuation: In about 5 years (2020), they reached a $1 billion valuation after raising $150 million from L Catterton.  
* Total Growth: After the seed, they raised a modest Series A (\~$12–15 million) and scaled rapidly with viral DTC, driven by social media and custom packaging.  
* Subscription Growth: Year 1 was small, but by year 3, tens of millions in revenue, and by year 5, over $100 million annually.

Third, Care/of:

* Seed: They raised about $2–3 million in early seed funding in 2016\.  
* Series A: They raised about $12 million shortly after.  
* Time to Valuation: Within 4 years, they reached $100 million+ in revenue and were acquired by Bayer in 2020\.  
* Total Growth: After the seed, they scaled mostly through DTC quizzes and subscription vitamin packs. By year 5, they had strong recurring revenue with mass-market appeal.

Finally, Curology:

* Seed: They raised about $15 million in their early rounds, as they were adding telehealth and clinical aspects.  
* Time to Valuation: They didn’t go public, but after about 6 years (around 2020), they merged into the Hims ecosystem and were generating hundreds of millions in revenue.  
* Subscription Growth: From the start, their model combined daily subscription skincare with telehealth. By year 3, they scaled to hundreds of thousands of users, and by year 5, they had a massive base inside Hims.

In summary, the pattern is consistent: each of these companies proved their model with relatively modest seed capital, scaled fast with a well-funded Series A, and turned personalization plus subscription into a category-defining business. This is the blueprint you’re following, but you’re starting with a stronger personalization engine and a higher-frequency daily ritual.
